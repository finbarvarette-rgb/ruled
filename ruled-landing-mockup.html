What's a demand letter?

A demand letter is a formal legal document you send to whoever owes you money. It clearly states what they owe, why they need to pay, and that you will be filing in small claims court if they don't respond within a set timeline.

It's your first move. It's professional. And it works more often than you'd think.

40%

of cases are resolved by a demand letter alone — before ever going to court.

One letter. That's often all it takes. Ruled builds it for you in minutes — personalized to your case, province-specific, and ready to send.

Questions

Common questions.

Is this legal advice?
No. Ruled provides legal information, not legal advice. We are not a law firm. Our tools help you understand your situation and prepare documents — the decisions are always yours.
What if I lose in court?
In small claims court, each party generally pays their own costs regardless of outcome. You won't be on the hook for the other side's legal fees in most cases.
How long does it take?
Your free case assessment takes about 3 minutes. Your demand letter is ready within minutes of purchase. Full case pack preparation typically takes 5–10 minutes.
Which provinces are supported?
All 10 Canadian provinces. Every assessment and document is tailored to your specific province's rules, limits, forms, and filing process.
What if they don't respond to the demand letter?
If they don't respond within the timeline, you move to file in small claims court. Our Full Case Pack gives you everything you need — filing instructions, court documents, and hearing preparation.
Is my information private?
Yes. Your case details are stored securely and never shared. Only you can access your assessments and documents through your account.
Do I need a lawyer?
No. Small claims court is specifically designed for self-represented parties. Ruled gives you everything a lawyer would prepare — at a fraction of the cost.
How long does the whole process take?
A demand letter gives the other party 14 days to respond. If you proceed to court, small claims hearings are typically scheduled within 3–6 months of filing, depending on your province.
